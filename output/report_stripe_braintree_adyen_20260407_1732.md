## Executive Summary

Stripe leads on overall digital presence, developer mindshare, product breadth, and innovation velocity, with the strongest SEO footprint and the most sophisticated content engine across web and social. Adyen leads in enterprise-grade global unified commerce credibility, especially for multinational merchants needing tightly integrated online and in-person payments with broad international coverage. Braintree remains competitive on enterprise processing and wallet distribution through PayPal/Venmo, but its market narrative is less distinct as the brand increasingly functions inside PayPal’s broader merchant stack.

The biggest competitive threat is Stripe, because it is expanding beyond core payment processing into AI-native checkout, agentic commerce, stablecoin infrastructure, and broader financial tooling while maintaining strong self-serve adoption. The clearest market opportunity is a simpler, more transparent global PSP offering for mid-market merchants that combines Adyen-like international strength with Stripe-like usability and clearer enterprise pricing. A second major opportunity is differentiated thought leadership and category education, where Braintree in particular is under-invested and Adyen is strong but less culturally resonant than Stripe.

## 2. Social Media Presence

### Stripe
- **Primary platforms:** LinkedIn, X, YouTube
- **Approx. audience:**
  - LinkedIn: ~700K–900K
  - X: ~250K–400K
  - YouTube: 100K+ likely
- **Posting style:** High-polish B2B brand publishing with strong editorial production values. Mix of product launches, customer stories, founder/startup content, macro internet economy commentary, and event-driven programming.
- **Standout content:**
  - Long-form leadership conversations perform especially well.
  - Examples cited:
    - *A conversation with Jony Ive* — ~849K views
    - *A conversation with NVIDIA’s Jensen Huang* — ~453K views
    - *A conversation with Mark Zuckerberg* — ~154K views
- **Assessment:** Strongest social presence of the three. Stripe behaves more like a premium media-tech brand than a typical PSP.

### Braintree
- **Primary platforms:** LinkedIn, X, YouTube
- **Approx. audience:**
  - LinkedIn: ~100K–250K
  - X: ~50K–120K
  - YouTube: low thousands to low tens of thousands
- **Posting style:** More practical and capability-led. Focus on payment basics, merchant enablement, wallets, checkout, and partner/customer proof.
- **Standout content:**
  - Older evergreen education performed best, e.g. *Payment Basics: Payments 101* at ~45K views.
  - Much of the video footprint appears older, with lower recent momentum.
- **Assessment:** Functional presence, but less differentiated and less active than Stripe. Brand voice is comparatively muted.

### Adyen
- **Primary platforms:** LinkedIn, X, YouTube
- **Approx. audience:**
  - LinkedIn: ~250K–450K
  - X: ~40K–100K
  - YouTube: relatively small vs. Stripe
